Profile

Andrew has worked in the IT industry for eighteen years, in the retail, banking, transport and e-commerce sectors in a variety of leadership roles. Andrew is an experienced technical architect taking systems from initial requirement, through design and implementation, onto deployment and support. Andrew is an adept software designer, with particular knowledge of object oriented design techniques and brings a wealth of Java development experience. Andrew has excellent database skills, with experience using DB2, Oracle and SQLServer.

As a manager Andrew has lead large development teams of up to 30 developers. He is passionate about agile development and has successfully introduced SCRUM and Extreme Programming across the Hut Group’s IT department. Andrew has successfully built relationships with business stakeholders at all levels within an organisation to deliver successful projects to the business.

The Laterooms Group

Technical Architect

Andrew is Solutions Architect within the LateRooms IT team. He works with the business to deliver solutions to support their strategic initiatives. Andrew takes a solution from the initial requirement, through system design and works with the development team to implement it. In particular Andrew defines the non-functional requirements of a system and ensures that these are delivered.

Andrew’s particular area of responsibility is the LateRooms API platform. The platform is use by both external partners and internal teams to deliver solutions to customers. Andrew ran the RFP to select an API management solution and leads the implementation of this solution.

William Morrisons PLC

Technical Architect

Andrew is a Technical Architect within the Solutions Architecture and Innovation team. As a Solutions Architect he works with people across business to develop robust solutions to enable the continued growth of the Morrisons online business.

As an innovator Andrew looks at how the business can use new technology to drive an enhanced user experience and growth. His most recent work has been to look at how the business can use social media, in particular Facebook, to engage with customers.

The Hut Group

Technical Architect

Andrew was a Technical Architect within the IT department taking the technical lead of a number of projects within the development portfolio. Andrew specialized in delivering complex integration projects, working with both suppliers and partners to deliver scalable solutions to the business.

Head of Development

Andrew was the Head of Development for the Hut Group where he lead a team of 30 developers through a period of massive growth. Andrew introduced Agile development to the team and business and successfully delivered a number of key projects to support the continued growth of the group. Andrew was accountable for the delivery of a portfolio of projects across multiple business streams, working with business stakeholders to prioritize requirements to deliver maximum value.

Andrew ensured that the team continually delivered successful projects to the business by:

  • Working with stakeholders to define a portfolio of projects to deliver value to the business.
  • Ensuring that the development process is constantly evolving to enable continued delivery.
  • Enabling the cadence of the team by facilitating daily stand-ups, planning meetings, user showcases and retrospectives.
  • Maintaining both the short-term project plans, as well the long-term forward plan.
  • Engaging with stakeholders to ensure that they are always informed as to the progress of the projects.

Atos Origin

Large Government Project: Technical Architect

Andrew joined the project as the configuration manager and was responsible for designing and building the configuration management system used by the project. Andrew also acted as transition manager, managing the hand over of the project to the customer’s production environment. In January 2007 Andrew was promoted to Technical Architect and Technical Team Lead for two of the systems developed as part of the project. Andrew transformed the user’s business requirements into a technical specification for implementation by the development team. Andrew was responsible for defining the software architecture of the system and managing the technical risks to the project. In addition Andrew ensured that the RUP processes were followed correctly. Andrew was responsible for the day-to-day leadership of a team of six developers and designers. He ensured that the designs produced by the team followed both the architecture of the system and the customer’s overall architecture. The solutions delivered by Andrew made extensive use of J2EE, in particular JMS messaging and EJBs. The GUI developed was struts based, making use of Java script and AJAX.

Customer Case Management: Technical Architect, Technical Team Leader

Andrew joined the CCM project as a technical architect and team leader. He produced the technical specification of the system including the software architecture, high-level component design and also the detailed design of the key components. The system was based around customizable framework bought from a specialist vendor and made heavy use of the struts framework. In addition the solution used a number of J2EE technologies including JMS, EJBs and JDBC. As the team leader, Andrew provided technical support to the team of 10 developers. Andrew was responsible for monitoring the progress of the team, assigning work to the developers and ensuring that the team delivered a high quality system on time and to budget.

Guernsey Integrated Social Security System (GISSS): Configuration Manager, Development Team Lead

Andrew was the Configuration Manager for the GISSS project. He was responsible for designing, building and running the configuration management system used by the project development team. Andrew maintained all the project’s development and system test environments and was responsible for building all the releases to system test and UAT.

Andrew also designed a number of benefit implementations. He took the use cases from the project analyst and used object oriented design techniques to produce a detailed design to be coded by a developer. These designs were documented using UML. Andrew shared the development team leading responsibilities on the project. This meant that he mentored junior members of the project team, performed code reviews and provided technical guidance for the whole project.

IBM Global Services

IT Specialist

IBM Global Services are the world’s largest systems integrator. Andrew’s first project at IBM was supporting the store ordering system used by ASDA. This taught him the importance of building maintainable code to ease the support of a live system. Andrew also had to work with staff in store, communicating complex technical ideas to people from a business background. Andrew later moved into a development role, and he spent a year in Belgium working on a very large real-time foreign exchange trading system.

Barclays Bank

Junior Developer

At Barclays Andrew was a developer on a team building common software components that were used by the other development teams within the bank.
